Here are some treasures from our box of old audio cassette tapes.

Newly Discovered Violin Music

The first track is a recording my dad made on September 17, 1942 with pianist Ralph Sunden. I found it at the very end of a cassette tape labeled, "Tape Of Old 78's For Martin Sauser." Dad never mentioned this recording, but I speculate it was fortuitously included in a box of old 78 r.p.m. records he obtained from his mother and had someone transfer to cassette tape. The collection includes many Fritz Kreisler melodies my dad loved; in my imagination these records filled the Sauser family home with music during my dad's childhood.

The 1942 recording is likely the only one Dad made before being drafted in 1943. If he had not survived the war, his mother would have had only this one recording to remember his playing, and indeed his voice, at age 19.

The second track is the beautiful slow movement of the Mendelssohn violin concerto. I found it on an undated tape of rather somber organ and violin music that must have been the prelude to a funeral service. Dad played the Mendelssohn concerto on February 19, 1948 with the Arkansas State Symphony Orchestra, which coincides with his early courtship of my mother. In my imagination, she attended the concert and heard him play this beautiful melody.

Martin's Wartime Experiences

In May 2000, our son, Jeff, interviewed his grandfather for a high school oral history project. Topics ranged from the effect of the Depression on my dad's family, to his experiences serving at the 231st Station Hospital in Norwich, England.

While some of the stories might be familiar (here's a link to Dad's written memoirs), there are some details in this recording I've never heard before. (By the way, Jeff got a 100 on his oral history project!)

Druid Hills Presbyterian Church Centennial Services

Druid Hills Presbyterian Church in Atlanta celebrated its centennial year in 1983. In this church where I was born and raised, where my dad played the violin at Sunday services for 50 years, and where my sisters and I were all married, the lead-up to the centennial celebration included these two services, recorded in 1981.

The October 11 service was led by a guest minister, Rev. Dr. Richard Dodds, preaching a sermon about predestination. Druid Hills' current minister at the time, Rev. Dr. Michal Hall, preached an uplifting sermon at the October 18 service.

Music includes organ and violin preludes (played by organist Porter Remington and my dad), hymns, and choir anthems including a beautful solo by tenor Charles Middleton. Unfortunately some of the music is abruptly truncated, but it's worth preserving as a tribute to everyone involved in the music program and the overall ministry of Druid Hills in those years.

Jimmy Carter's Sunday School Class

Jill, Jeff, Heidi and I visited Maranatha Baptist Church in Plains, GA on April 11, 1999. We were honored to hear Jimmy Carter teach Sunday School and have our picture taken with him. I ordered a cassette tape for the day we attended; it also included his lesson for the Sunday prior to our visit, which happened to be Easter Sunday.

The third track is a WABE radio program, circa 1994, discussing Carter's published book of poems, Always a Reckoning. I recorded the program off the air and missed a few minutes at the beginning.
